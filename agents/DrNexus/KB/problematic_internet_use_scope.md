# Problematic Internet Use: Scope Brief

## Working Scope
This swarm studies problematic internet use (PIU) as a psychiatric and behavioral-science topic. The working scope includes broad problematic internet use and specific internet-use disorders when relevant, especially internet gaming disorder (IGD).

## Guardrails
-   Distinguish high engagement from clinically impairing use.
-   Treat screening scores, symptom scales, and formal diagnoses as different levels of evidence.
-   Be explicit when evidence comes from adolescent samples, convenience samples, or gaming-specific cohorts.

## Emerging Domain: AI-Chatbot and LLM Use as a PIU-Adjacent Construct

As of 2025–2026, a body of peer-reviewed and preprint literature has begun testing whether LLM-based tools (general-purpose chatbots, AI companions, coding assistants) generate dependence-like patterns that overlap with PIU. This domain is conceptually unstable: multiple scales exist, construct boundaries with internet addiction and smartphone addiction remain unclear, and the evidence is uneven across use types (companion, task-based, occupational).

### Scope Note
-   AI-chatbot dependence is not yet a recognized formal diagnosis in major nosologies.
-   Multiple 2025 scales exist (CAI dependence scale, LLM-D12) but require independent replication.
-   The strongest dependence-adjacent evidence comes from companion and general-purpose chatbot use, not coding-agent use specifically.
-   For coding agents, the literature supports overreliance and reduced cognitive engagement more clearly than PIU/addiction as a clinical construct.
-   Apply the standard PIU guardrails: distinguish high engagement from clinically impairing use; treat scale scores as screening proxies, not diagnoses.
