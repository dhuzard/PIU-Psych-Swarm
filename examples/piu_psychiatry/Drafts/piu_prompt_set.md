# PIU Prompt Set

## Scoping Review Prompts

### Broad PIU overview
Review the psychiatric literature on problematic internet use in adolescents. Separate prevalence, diagnostic ambiguity, comorbidity, mechanisms, and interventions. Include a short limitations section and a references list.

### University students
Create a literature map of problematic internet use among university students, emphasizing depression, anxiety, sleep disturbance, and measurement heterogeneity.

### Clinical differentiation
Compare problematic internet use, internet addiction, and internet gaming disorder as clinical or research constructs. Clarify where formal disorder recognition exists and where it does not.

## Focused Evidence Brief Prompts

### Sleep
Draft a neutral evidence brief on the association between problematic internet use and sleep problems in adolescents, including strength of evidence and key caveats.

### Mechanisms
Summarize the main neurocognitive and theoretical models of problematic internet use, including I-PACE, cue-reactivity, reward sensitivity, and executive-control findings.

### Intervention evidence
Review the evidence for CBT, family-based interventions, school-based prevention, and pharmacotherapy for internet gaming disorder or problematic internet use in youth.

## Journalism / Policy Prompts

### Public-facing brief
Write a concise public-facing brief on why problematic internet use is a psychiatric research topic, while clearly distinguishing heavy use from clinically impairing use.

### Service planning brief
Prepare a neutral briefing for a university mental health service on what is currently known about problematic internet use in students and what remains uncertain.
