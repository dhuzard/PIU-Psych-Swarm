# Adversity Criteria in Preclinical Toxicology: Anchor References

## Core Problem
"Adverse effect" is a regulatory and scientific construct, not a raw measurement. Different frameworks define adversity differently, and the criteria applied must be cited explicitly in any adversity judgment.

## Key Frameworks

### Frank et al. 2012 (STP Position Paper)
-   Defines adverse effect as a change that causes functional impairment, damage, or increased susceptibility to harm.
-   Distinguishes adaptive changes (non-adverse: enzyme induction, compensatory hypertrophy) from adverse changes (cell death, organ dysfunction, irreversibility).
-   Reference: Sellers RS, Morton D, Michael B, et al. Society of Toxicologic Pathology Position Paper: Organ Weight Recommendations for Toxicology Studies. Toxicol Pathol. 2007;35(5):751-755. [Confirm Frank 2012 citation via LitScout]

### EPA (U.S. Environmental Protection Agency)
-   Defines adverse effect in the context of risk assessment: any change in morphology, physiology, growth, development, reproduction, or lifespan that results in impairment of functional capacity or impairment of capacity to compensate for additional stress.

### EMA CHMP
-   EMA/CHMP/SWP/28367/07 — Guidance on the non-clinical documentation for mixed marketing authorisation applications. Adversity addressed in context of safety margin derivation.

### ICH S1C(R2)
-   Dose selection for carcinogenicity studies — uses adversity framing in the context of maximum tolerated dose.

## Common Adaptive vs. Adverse Distinctions
| Finding | Typically Adaptive | Typically Adverse |
|---|---|---|
| Liver weight increase | Enzyme induction (reversible, no histopath) | Hepatocyte necrosis, fibrosis |
| ALT increase | Mild transient (≤3× ULN, reversible) | Sustained ≥5–10× ULN, with morphology |
| Thyroid weight increase | TSH-driven compensatory (rodent-specific) | Follicular cell necrosis |
| Adrenal weight increase | Stress response | Cortical necrosis, hemorrhage |

## TOCSAS Rule
All adversity claims in TOCSAS outputs must carry:
1. The explicit criteria applied (framework name and reference).
2. The specific finding characteristics that triggered the adversity judgment.
3. Whether the judgment is the original study authors' or the TOCSAS analyst's.
